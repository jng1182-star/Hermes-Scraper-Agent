SOV Index Calculation

            
The Composite Share-of-Voice (SOV) Index is a directional, indexed metric — not actual ad spend. It is calculated from six observable signals, weighted as follows:

            
    
              
  • Creative Volume (30%) — Share of active ad/post count within the competitor set per platform.
    • 
              
  • Creative Velocity (10%) — Share of new ads launched in the last 7 days (pace of creative refresh).
    • 
              
  • Ad Longevity (15%) — Normalized average days since earliest ad start date; measures campaign persistence.
    • 
              
  • Platform Presence (15%) — Number of active platforms relative to the most platform-active brand in the set.
    • 
              
  • Reach Bucket (15%) — Normalized impression tier derived from ad library reach ranges.
    • 
              
  • Engagement Corroboration (15%) — Engagement rate benchmarked against industry averages; secondary signal only.
    • 
            

            
All signals are normalized 0–100 across the competitor set. The composite index is the weighted sum. Values sum to ~100 across all brands for a given platform.


            
Data Sources

            
    
              
  • Meta Ad Library API — Confirmed paid posts on Facebook and Instagram. Highest reliability for Meta platforms.
    • 
              
  • YouTube Data API v3 — Channel-level metrics (subscribers, views, engagement).
    • 
              
  • TikTok Commercial Content Library — Paid ad data. Coverage limited to EU-targeted campaigns; APAC-only campaigns may yield no data.
    • 
              
  • Engagement baseline fallback (Route 2) — Where ad library data is unavailable, posts with >2× median engagement are classified as "Paid (Est.)" with Medium confidence.
    • 
            


            
Instagram Note

            
Instagram data is modelled from the associated Facebook Page via Meta Ad Library. Meta's ad library covers both Facebook and Instagram surfaces under the same advertiser. Instagram entries are labelled "(modelled)" throughout this report and should be interpreted as directional only.


            
TikTok Suppression

            
If fewer than 2 TikTok posts are detected for a given market, TikTok is excluded from the composite SOV formula and the remaining platform weights are re-normalized proportionally. This is noted explicitly wherever TikTok has been suppressed. This approach prevents misleading zero-values from inflating competitor comparisons.


            
Paid Classification Labels

            
    
              
  • Paid (Confirmed) — Post found in an official ad library. High confidence.
    • 
              
  • Paid (Est.) — Post not in ad library but engagement >2× brand median OR in top 10% of brand posts. Medium confidence. Marked with ~ indicator.
    • 
              
  • Organic — No paid signals detected. High confidence classification by exclusion.
    • 
            


            
Scope & Limitations

            
All SOV figures reflect presence within the selected competitor group only — not total industry or market share. This is a directional tool for competitive benchmarking. It does not provide actual spend figures, and should not be used to estimate media investment.
